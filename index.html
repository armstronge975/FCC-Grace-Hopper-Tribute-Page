Grace Hopper

US Navy Rear Admiral

Creator of the Compiler

1906 - 1992

Timeline

  • 1906 - Born on December 9 in New York City
  • 1928 - Graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Vassar College with a Bachelor's in mathematics and history.
  • 1930 - Graduated from Yale University with a Master's in mathematics; married Vincent Foster Hopper
  • 1931 - Began teaching at Yale
  • 1934 - Graduated from Yale with a Ph.D. in mathematics
  • 1943 - Left teaching and joined the US Naval Reserve
  • 1944 - Became a lieutenant and programmed the Mark I, the first computer, at Harvard. She later went on to work on the Mark II and Mark III, and to coin the term "bug".
  • 1949 - Went into private industry; worked at Eckert-Mauchly Computer Corporation and UNIVAC
  • 1952 - Invented the first ever compilier
  • 1959 - Consulted committee at CODASYL conference to create the COBOL programming language
  • 1966 - Retired from the Naval Reserve, but was called for active duty a year later
  • 1971 - ACM created the annual Grace Muray Hopper Award for Outstanding Young Computer Professionals
  • 1973 - First woman and American to become a Distinguished Fellow of the British Computer Society
  • 1986 - Officially retired from the Navy
  • 1991 - Received the National Medal of Technology
  • 1992 - Died on January 1 in Arlington, Virginia
